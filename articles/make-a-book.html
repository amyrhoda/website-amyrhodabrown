How to Make a Book

Like many things — croissants, shoes, boats — books are harder to put together than they seem. And like many things, the first 50% of the job (the writing) takes 90% of the time, and the second 50% also takes 90% of the time.

Here are the minimum steps required (in my opinion) to create a good self-published non-fiction book:

  • writing, optionally including:
    • developmental editing
    • incorporating feedback from beta readers
  • stylistic editing
  • creation of front matter according to publishing conventions (table of contents, copyright page, dedication, acknowledgments, etc.), back matter, and cover copy
  • copyediting
  • typesetting
  • indexing
  • cover design
  • proofreading

Not to mention:

  • dealing with the print-on-demand company or printer, who will have their own requirements for file types, book metadata, and so on
  • ebook production for several different devices
  • marketing and promotion

At a publishing company, ten, twenty or more people might work on your book through the various stages. As a self-publisher you probably can't afford to hire ten or twenty people, but you should certainly hire one or two: an editor for sure, an indexer if you want a good index, a proofreader to check your final proof.
