Editing and Custom Book Production

About Amy

A few years ago I had just sent my youngest child off to kindergarten, and I had some time on my hands. My friend Greg Wilson had the opposite problem — too much to do, not enough hands — so he asked me to help him project-edit, and eventually self-publish, a book called The Architecture of Open Source Applications. I enjoyed the process so much that I collaborated on the second volume, and decided to make editing and self-publishing my career.

I love to read, and I love books, not just because they contain lots of words to read, but as objects in their own right. I like the way they feel and look, and I like all the decisions that go into making a book: how big should it be, what goes on the cover, what typefaces should we use? I like deciphering the subtext of those choices.

I also love science: the process of it and the truths that it reveals. I like knowing new things, but I also enjoy that state of not knowing, when we have some hypotheses but we're not sure which one is right. I also like that feeling when you find out that something you thought was true isn't, and the new truth makes so much more sense and you realize (or you tell yourself) that you were never really comfortable with the old truth after all.

I believe science is the only way we're going to fix the problems of the world, and it's important that my work contributes to the solutions.

I live in Toronto with my husband and two daughters. When I'm not helping people communicate, I read (for fun), sing, walk, bake, and hang out with my kids.
